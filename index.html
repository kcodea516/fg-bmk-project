Abstract

Recent advancements in Large Vision-Language Models (LVLMs) have demonstrated remarkable multimodal perception capabilities, garnering significant attention. While numerous evaluation studies have emerged, assessing LVLMs both holistically and on specialized tasks, fine-grained image tasks—fundamental to computer vision—remain largely unexplored. To fill this gap, we introduce a comprehensive fine-grained evaluation benchmark, i.e., FG-BMK, comprising 1.01 million questions and 0.28 million images. Our evaluation systematically examines LVLMs from both human-oriented and machine-oriented perspectives, focusing on their semantic recognition and fine-grained feature representation capabilities. Through extensive experiments on twelve representative LVLMs/VLMs, we uncover key findings regarding the influence of training paradigms, modality alignment, perturbation susceptibility, and fine-grained category reasoning on task performance. This work provides critical insights into the limitations of current LVLMs and offers guidance for future data construction and model design in the development of more advanced LVLMs. Our code is open-source and available at https://github.com/SEU-VIPGroup/FG-BMK.

Why FG-BMK?

Bridging the Alignment Gap in Granularity Inconsistencies

Current multimodal models and datasets often exhibit severe granularity inconsistencies. When presented with fine-grained visual inputs, standard data generation pipelines and models naturally prioritize generic, coarse-grained objects (e.g., "bird", "dog", "cat") instead of specific sub-categories or species. FG-BMK constructs rigorously aligned fine-grained samples to evaluate and mitigate this "Alignment Gap."

Evaluation Framework

To comprehensively assess Large Vision-Language Models (LVLMs) in fine-grained scenarios, FG-BMK introduces a dual evaluation paradigm. This framework bridges the gap between how humans perceive visual information and how machines perceive visual features, revealing the critical "Alignment Gap" in current multimodal models.

1. Human-oriented Evaluation

Focus: Semantic Recognition & Interaction

This paradigm employs dialogue-like interactions to simulate real-world human queries. We evaluate models through:

  • Attribute Recognition: Testing sensitivity to minute visual details (color, shape).
  • Knowledge Bias: Identifying if models rely on prior "guessing" rather than actual visual cues.
  • Hierarchical Reasoning: Assessing logical category depth (Class → Genus → Species).

Core Discovery: Measures how well the model "understands" and communicates fine-grained semantics in conversational AI.

2. Machine-oriented Evaluation

Focus: Feature Representation Strength

This paradigm directly measures the model's underlying visual backbone using objective vision tasks:

  • Fine-grained Retrieval: Finding specific species among highly similar categories.
  • Fine-grained Classification: Forcing Distinguishing between visually indistinguishable samples.

By bypassing the language generation layer, we isolate the model's pure visual perception capabilities.

Core Discovery: Reveals the "Alignment Gap" between frozen visual encoders and multimodal projectors.
